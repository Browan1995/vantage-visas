The 10 best destinations for South African passport holders — with real 2026 entry requirements.

1 Visa-Free
🇹🇭

Thailand

60-day visa-free access. Digital Arrival Card (TM6) required before departure — complete it online.

60 Days Digital Arrival Card

🇹🇭 Thailand — 60-Day Visa-Free (2026)

  • SA passport valid for 6+ months beyond entry date
  • Digital Arrival Card (TM6) completed online before departure
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, Airbnb, host letter)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (approx. THB 20,000 / ~R8,500 per person)

Key Notes

⚠️ The old paper TM6 card has been replaced by an online digital arrival card. Complete it at thailand.go.th/travel-card before you fly. Airlines are checking compliance at check-in.

2 ETA Required
🇬🇧

United Kingdom

ETA mandatory for short visits since January 2025. Standard Visitor Visa required for stays over 6 months.

ETA / Standard Visa Mandatory ETA

🇬🇧 United Kingdom — ETA (2026)

Since January 2025, all South African nationals require a UK Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) for short stays (tourism, business, transit). This replaced the previous visa-free access.

  • UK ETA applied for via the UK ETA app or uk.gov website (approx. £10)
  • Valid SA passport
  • Return or onward travel confirmation
  • Hotel/accommodation details
  • Bank statements showing sufficient funds

Standard Visitor Visa (6-month stays)

  • All of the above, plus:
  • Employer letter / proof of business ownership
  • 3–6 months bank statements
  • Proof of property / assets in SA (to demonstrate ties)
⚠️ The ETA is NOT a visa. It allows entry but immigration officers retain the right to refuse entry. Apply at least 72 hours before departure — most are approved within minutes, but allow more time.

3 Interview Required
🇺🇸

USA

B1/B2 10-year tourist and business visa. Consular interview required. Approval rates are high with correct preparation.

10-Year Validity B1/B2 Visa

🇺🇸 USA — B1/B2 Visa

  • DS-160 online nonimmigrant visa application
  • Valid SA passport (6+ months validity)
  • US$185 visa application fee (MRV fee) — non-refundable
  • Consular interview at the US Embassy in Pretoria or Cape Town
  • Bank statements (3–6 months, showing consistent income/savings)
  • Proof of employment or business ownership
  • Proof of SA ties (property, family, job letter)
  • Travel itinerary and accommodation bookings
  • Previous travel history (strengthens your application)
⚠️ If you've previously been denied a US visa, you MUST disclose this on the DS-160. Concealment is grounds for permanent ban. Vantage Visas specialises in post-denial reapplications.

4 EES Active
🇫🇷🇮🇹

Schengen (France / Italy)

Type C Short Stay Visa. Entry/Exit System (EES) fully active from April 2026 — biometrics required at all borders.

90 Days / 180 Days EES Active

🇪🇺 Schengen Type C Short Stay Visa (2026)

  • Schengen visa application form (completed at VFS Global or embassy)
  • Valid SA passport (3+ months beyond stay, 2 blank pages minimum)
  • Two recent passport-size photographs (biometric, white background)
  • Travel insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage, valid in all Schengen states)
  • Return flight bookings
  • Confirmed accommodation (hotel reservations or host letter)
  • 3–6 months bank statements (minimum R5,000/day of stay recommended)
  • Employer letter or proof of business (leave approval, registration documents)

⚠️ EES — Entry/Exit System (Active April 2026)

The EU's new Entry/Exit System replaces passport stamps. Your biometrics (fingerprints + photo) will be captured at your FIRST Schengen border crossing. This is mandatory. The system tracks your 90/180-day allowance digitally. Overstaying will now be automatically flagged and can result in a 3–5 year ban from the EU.

5 Visa-Free
🇲🇺

Mauritius

90-day visa-free for SA passport holders. One of the most straightforward destinations for Saffas.

90 Days No Visa Needed

🇲🇺 Mauritius — 90-Day Visa-Free

  • Valid SA passport
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Proof of sufficient funds (MUR 3,500 / ~R1,400 per day)
✅ No visa required! Immigration officers may ask for bank statements and hotel bookings at the border. Have printed copies ready.
6 eVisa
🇦🇪

UAE (Dubai)

eVisa available for 30 or 60 days. Simple online process — usually approved within 48 hours.

30 / 60 Days eVisa

🇦🇪 UAE — eVisa (30 or 60 Days)

  • Valid SA passport (6+ months validity, 2 blank pages)
  • Colour passport photograph (white background)
  • Completed eVisa application via ICP or sponsor
  • Return flight booking
  • Hotel reservation or sponsor letter
  • Bank statement (last 3 months)
✅ Most approvals come within 24–48 hours. The 30-day visa can be extended once from inside the UAE for a further 30 days. The 60-day visa cannot be extended.
7 Subclass 600
🇦🇺

Australia

Subclass 600 Visitor Visa — fully online application. Typically processed in 2–4 weeks, sometimes faster.

Up to 3 Months Online eVisa

🇦🇺 Australia — Subclass 600 Visitor Visa

  • Valid SA passport
  • Completed ImmiAccount online application
  • Current passport-size photograph
  • Bank statements (3–6 months)
  • Proof of employment / income
  • Return flight booking
  • Travel itinerary and accommodation
  • Health insurance for the duration of your stay
⚠️ Australia requires health examinations for some applicants (usually for stays over 3 months). Ensure your application is complete — missing documents are the most common cause of delays.
8 Visa on Arrival
🇮🇩

Bali (Indonesia)

Visa on Arrival at the airport. 30 days, extendable once. Simple and fast — just bring cash USD.

30 Days + Extension VoA USD 35

🇮🇩 Bali / Indonesia — Visa on Arrival

  • Valid SA passport (6+ months validity, 1 blank page)
  • USD 35 cash (exact or close to it — the VoA booth prefers USD)
  • Return or onward ticket out of Indonesia
  • Proof of accommodation (for immigration check)
✅ Alternatively, apply for an e-VOA (online Visa on Arrival) before departure at molina.imigrasi.go.id to skip the airport queue. Cost is the same. Highly recommended during peak season when queues can be 60+ minutes.
9 eVisa
🇹🇷

Turkey

Simple eVisa via the official evisa.gov.tr portal. Usually approved within minutes. 30 days, multiple entry.

30 Days eVisa — Fast

🇹🇷 Turkey — eVisa

  • Valid SA passport (6+ months validity)
  • Apply online at evisa.gov.tr (approx. USD 50, credit card required)
  • Valid email address for delivery
  • Return flight and accommodation (for immigration checks)
✅ Most SA passport holders receive instant approval. Print or download the eVisa to your phone before departure — immigration may request a physical copy.
10 D7 / DN Visa
🇵🇹

Portugal

Tourist entry via Schengen Type C. For longer stays: the D7 Passive Income Visa or Digital Nomad Visa (D8) are top Saffa favourites for 2026.

90 Days Tourist D7 / D8 Residency

🇵🇹 Portugal — Tourist & Long-Stay Options

Tourist (Schengen Type C): Standard Schengen visa applies — see France/Italy entry above. The EES biometric system is also active at Portuguese borders from April 2026.

D7 — Passive Income Visa

  • Minimum monthly passive income of ~€760 (pension, rental, investments)
  • Proof of accommodation in Portugal (12-month rental or purchase)
  • Criminal record check from SA Police (SAPS) — apostilled
  • Bank statements proving income consistency
  • Appointment at Portuguese consulate in Pretoria or Cape Town

D8 — Digital Nomad Visa

  • Minimum monthly income of €3,040 (4× Portuguese minimum wage)
  • Employment contract or client contracts (for freelancers)
  • All standard D7 documentation above
⚠️ AIMA (formerly SEF) appointment backlogs can be 3–6 months. Apply early. Vantage Visas has Portuguese immigration partners who can significantly accelerate your timeline.

Visa Types

The Two Visas Every SA Crew Member Needs to Know

🇺🇸 US B1/B2 — Crew Endorsement

The most common visa for SA yacht crew working in the Caribbean, Florida, and US waters.

USA

The B1 (Business) component of the B1/B2 visa allows SA crew to legally work aboard a yacht in US waters, provided the vessel is foreign-flagged. This is the standard route for deckhands, stewardesses, engineers, and officers joining yachts in Fort Lauderdale, Miami, or the US Virgin Islands.

Document Checklist

  • DS-160 nonimmigrant visa application
  • Valid SA passport (6+ months)
  • US$185 application fee (non-refundable)
  • STCW Basic Safety Training Certificate
  • Seaman's Book (Continuous Discharge Book)
  • Contract of Employment (LOE from captain/owner)
  • Crew list / letter from vessel's captain
  • Details of the vessel (flag state, port of registry, IMO number)
  • Bank statements (crew may have modest savings — consulate understands)
  • Evidence of intent to return to SA (family, property, existing contracts)
  • SAMSA Certificate of Competency (if applicable)
  • First Aid / Medical training certificates
⚠️ Dockwalkers: Arriving in the US to look for crew work (dockwalking) on a tourist visa is technically a violation of US immigration law. Vantage Visas advises all clients on the correct legal route — which may include the B1 endorsement obtained in advance, or a specific crew visa strategy.

🇪🇺 Schengen Seafarer Visa (Type D / Crew Endorsement)

For SA crew joining vessels in the Mediterranean — from Monaco to Mallorca.

EU / Schengen

The Schengen Seafarer Visa allows SA crew to transit through or work in Schengen territory aboard a vessel. Issued by the flag state's consulate or the consulate of the principal port of destination. The visa is tied to the vessel and voyage, not general tourism.

Document Checklist

  • Schengen visa application (Category D for seafarers)
  • Valid SA passport (3+ months beyond stay)
  • STCW Basic Safety Training Certificate
  • Seaman's Book (Continuous Discharge Book)
  • Contract of Employment / Letter of Engagement
  • Vessel details (flag, IMO, port of registry)
  • Captain's letter confirming crew position
  • Travel insurance (€30,000 minimum cover)
  • Return flight booking to SA
  • SAMSA Certificate of Competency (if officer rank)
  • Yellow Fever certificate if transiting through Africa
  • Port clearance documents (if provided by captain)
⚠️ EES Active from April 2026: Even seafarers are subject to the EU's Entry/Exit System. Your biometrics will be captured at your first Schengen border crossing. The 90/180-day tourist allowance and the seafarer visa are tracked separately — consult Vantage Visas to ensure you're on the correct visa category.
