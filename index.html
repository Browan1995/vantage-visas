🇺🇸 US B1/B2 with Crew Endorsement

For SA crew joining or dockwalking in US waters — Fort Lauderdale, Newport, Annapolis.

USA

The B1/B2 visa with a crew endorsement is the industry-standard entry document for SA seafarers entering the US to work aboard a foreign-flagged vessel. The endorsement must be obtained during the consulate interview — it cannot be added later.

Document Checklist

  • Valid SA Passport (6+ months validity)
  • Completed DS-160 application form
  • SEVIS / MRV fee payment receipt
  • Seaman's Book (Continuous Discharge Book)
  • Contract of Employment (LOE from captain/owner)
  • Crew list / letter from vessel's captain
  • Details of the vessel (flag state, port of registry, IMO number)
  • Bank statements
  • Evidence of intent to return to SA
  • STCW Basic Safety Training Certificate
  • SAMSA Certificate of Competency (if applicable)
⚠️ Dockwalkers: Arriving in the US to look for crew work on a tourist visa is technically a violation of US immigration law. Vantage Visas advises all clients on the correct legal route — the B1 endorsement must be obtained in advance.

🇪🇺 Schengen Seafarer Visa (Type D / Crew Endorsement)

For SA crew joining vessels in the Mediterranean — Monaco, Antibes, Mallorca, Palma.

EU / Schengen

The Schengen Seafarer Visa allows SA crew to transit through or work in Schengen territory aboard a vessel. Issued by the flag state's consulate or the consulate of the principal port of destination. The visa is tied to the vessel and voyage, not general tourism.

Document Checklist

  • Schengen visa application (Category D for seafarers)
  • Valid SA passport (3+ months beyond stay)
  • STCW Basic Safety Training Certificate
  • Seaman's Book (Continuous Discharge Book)
  • Contract of Employment / Letter of Engagement
  • Vessel details (flag, IMO, port of registry)
  • Captain's letter confirming crew position
  • Travel insurance (€30,000 minimum cover)
  • Return flight booking to SA
  • SAMSA Certificate of Competency (if officer rank)
  • Yellow Fever certificate if transiting through Africa
  • Port clearance documents (if provided by captain)
⚠️ EES Active from April 2026: Even seafarers are subject to the EU's Entry/Exit System. Your biometrics will be captured at your first Schengen border crossing. Consult Vantage Visas to ensure you're on the correct visa category.
